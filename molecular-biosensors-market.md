<p><p>The molecular biosensors market encompasses various types, including electrochemical biosensors that detect analytes through changes in electrical signals, making them suitable for glucose monitoring and environmental testing. Optical biosensors utilize light interaction for precise measurements, often used in medical diagnostics. Thermal biosensors measure temperature changes to analyze biochemical reactions. Lastly, piezoelectric biosensors gauge mass changes on a sensor surface, ideal for detecting pathogens and toxins. Each type offers unique advantages, catering to diverse applications in healthcare, environmental monitoring, and food safety.</p></p>
