<p><p>The nickel mining market comprises primarily two types: sulfide ores, such as pentlandite, and laterite nickel ores. Pentlandite, found in sulfide deposits, is considered more efficient for nickel extraction due to its higher nickel content and lower processing costs. In contrast, laterite ores, typically found in tropical regions, require more complex processing methods like high-pressure acid leaching. Both types play crucial roles in supply, driven by varying demand, processing technologies, and market dynamics influenced by global economic conditions.</p></p>
