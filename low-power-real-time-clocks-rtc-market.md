<p><p>Low Power Real-time Clocks (RTCs) are specialized integrated circuits used to keep track of current time and date, consuming minimal power. The market is divided into two primary types: I2C-bus RTCs and SPI RTCs. I2C-bus RTCs utilize the I2C communication protocol, enabling multiple devices to communicate over a two-wire bus, ideal for low pin count applications. Conversely, SPI RTCs leverage the Serial Peripheral Interface for faster data transfer and simpler communication, making them suitable for high-speed applications requiring precise timing.</p></p>
